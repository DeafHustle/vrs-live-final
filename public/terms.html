4. VRI Services

4.1 Service Description

The Platform connects users with certified ASL interpreters for real-time Video Remote Interpreting sessions. Sessions are facilitated through WebRTC video technology with end-to-end encryption.

4.2 Service Availability

  • VRI services are available based on interpreter availability
  • We strive for 24/7 availability but cannot guarantee it, especially during our initial launch phase
  • Emergency situations requiring immediate on-site interpreting should use local emergency services or 911 Video Relay Service

4.3 Session Guidelines

  • Both users and interpreters may end a session at any time
  • Sessions are billed by the minute, with a minimum of 1 minute per session
  • Users must have a stable internet connection and working camera/microphone
  • Recording sessions is prohibited unless both parties provide written consent

4.4 Service Limitations

The Platform is not intended to replace:

  • Emergency 911 services or Telecommunications Relay Services (TRS)
  • In-person interpreting for medical procedures, legal proceedings, or other situations requiring physical presence
  • Certified Deaf Interpreters (CDI) for specialized communication needs
